xr laser tag

Turn any space into a laser tag arena with a single press

tl;dr

Lasertag (one word) is an experiment in reducing the friction of mixed reality games. The goal is to make something that is super flexible and 'just works.'

It requires little prior setup, progressively maps and syncs room 'geometry' (height, really) between all players over the network, and connects headsets to games automatically.

The game runs on Meta Quest 3/3s and is built on Unity using their first-party Netcode for GameObjects package. You can try the game here >>>

simple setup

In order to play, players must do the following:

  • 1. Everyone puts on their headset
  • 2. Everyone connects to the same wifi network
  • 3. Everyone launches the app
  • 4. One person presses the 'host' button

From there, all nearby headsets (should) connect and colocate automatically. Limitations are detailed in 'known issues.'

progressive network-synced environment mapping

The game uses Quest's built-in depth estimation API to progressively and collectively map the playspace for laser collision. Headsets stream their scan to other headsets and they all build a shared heightmap as they move around.

This way, even if Headset #1 isn't close enough to scan what Headset #2 is hiding behind, #1 will know something is in the way.

There is no 'environment mesh' (except for debug visuals).

depth lighting

Laser's lights are inverted sphere meshes with a depth/normal-sampling shader. They're like rudimentary deferred point lights.

These are expensive due to alpha blending and I still need to optimize them.

known issues

The game still has many problems I need to solve:

  • Headsets must be on the same subnet to connect.
    • I plan to fall back onto Unity's first party relay service when necessary.
  • Meta's shared anchors are finicky. Sometimes they won't upload or download.
    • Meta anchors are a cloud service and require a decent internet connection.
    • I plan to use keyboard tracking as an alternative colocation solution.
  • Players' virtual avatars will drift and dislocate from their real bodies over time.
  • Playspace map syncing will occasionally cause app crashes.
  • Depth-lights, being alpha-blended & often full-screen, are slow.
  • Distant lasers and avatars are invisible due to limited depth-occlusion range.
  • The playspace mapper will see and add players to the heightmap.
  • The mapper generally needs better filtering and validation.
  • All of the visuals are placeholders.
