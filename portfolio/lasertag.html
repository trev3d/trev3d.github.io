Lasertag

Turn any space into a laser tag arena with a single press

Lasertag (one word) is an experiment in reducing the friction & 'time to fun' of mixed reality games with something that 'just works.'

It progressively scans and syncs room geometry between all players without any 'room setup' process and connects nearby headsets automatically.

The game runs on Meta Quest 3/3s and is built on Unity using their first-party Netcode for GameObjects package.

Join the beta for Meta Quest 3/3s ➔

Simple setup

  • 1. Everyone puts on their headset
  • 2. Everyone connects to the same WiFi network
  • 3. Everyone launches the app
  • 4. One person presses the 'host' button

From there, all nearby headsets (should) connect and colocate automatically. Limitations are detailed in 'known issues.'

Room mapping

Progressive and network-synced

The app uses Quest's depth estimation to progressively map the playspace (rooms, walls, furniture, etc) for laser collision via compute kernels. Headsets stream their scans to other headsets and they all add to a shared heightmap.

This way, even if Headset #1 isn't close enough to scan what Headset #2 is hiding behind, #2 will tell #1 that something is there.

Depth lighting

Laser's lights are inverted sphere meshes with a depth/normal-sampling shader. They're like rudimentary deferred point lights.

These are expensive (alpha blended fragments that often overlap and cover the whole view) and I still need to optimize them.

Known issues

  • Moving too far will interrupt the game with a system pop-up.
  • Headsets must be on the same subnet to connect.
  • Meta's shared anchors are finicky and often fail.
  • Players' virtual avatars will drift and dislocate from their real bodies over time.
  • Playspace map syncing will occasionally cause crashes.
  • Depth-lights are unoptimized.
  • Distant lasers and avatars are invisible due to limited depth-occlusion range.
  • The playspace mapper will add other players' bodies to the heightmap.
  • The mapper generally needs better filtering and validation.
  • All of the visuals are placeholders.
