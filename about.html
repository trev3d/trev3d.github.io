My name is Julian Triveri. I'm a undergrad at Purdue University and an employee at a university run visualization lab. Several projects shown on this site come from my work there.

I have experience in realtime 3D art, basic animation, and high-level programming, and I'm currently expanding my skillset to include graphics and low-level programming.

While I believe that form should always follow function, I still have a keen interest in design and aesthetics. I especially love creating refined visuals for performace-constrained platforms such as web and mobile VR. Working within such constraints requires a more human touch where problem solving and clever tricks make all the difference.

If I can be candid and a little unprofessional for a moment, I want to admit that I often ask myself whether my work matters that much. The world is full of many important problems, few of which are solved by computer nerds who can make pretty pictures. If you are someone who is working on something important and impactful and my sort of work would somehow help your effort, please do not hesitate to contact me. I would rather sink my time and effort into things that truly matter.
